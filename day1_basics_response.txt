Question: A user reports that their Confluence Cloud space export is stuck. The progress bar reached 100% but no download link appeared. What are the most likely causes?

Answer:
The frustration of a stuck export! Don't worry, I'm here to help you troubleshoot the issue.

When a Confluence Cloud space export gets stuck at 100%, with no download link appearing, there are several possible causes:

1. **Network or internet connectivity issues**: Check if your network connection is stable and working properly. A slow or unreliable internet connection can cause the export process to timeout or fail.
2. **Large content size**: If you have a massive amount of content in your space (e.g., thousands of pages), it may take a long time to export, causing the progress bar to reach 100% without completing the download.
3. **Space permissions or access issues**: Ensure that you have the necessary permissions and access rights to export the space. Check if any users or groups are restricted from accessing certain content or spaces.
4. **Confluence Cloud server-side issues**: It's possible that there's a temporary issue with Confluence Cloud's servers, causing the export process to fail or get stuck. Try waiting for some time (e.g., 30 minutes) and then retry the export.
5. **Browser or plugin issues**: Check if your browser is up-to-date and functioning correctly. Some users have reported issues with certain browsers or plugins interfering with Confluence Cloud exports.
6. **Export settings or options**: Review your export settings to ensure you haven't selected an option that's causing the issue (e.g., exporting specific content types, like attachments).
7. **Atlassian maintenance or downtime**: Occasionally, Atlassian may perform scheduled maintenance or experience downtime, which can affect Confluence Cloud services, including exports.

To troubleshoot further:

1. Check the Confluence Cloud status page for any reported issues or maintenance notifications.
2. Try restarting your browser and retrying the export.
3. Contact your organization's IT department or Confluence administrator to see if they've experienced similar issues or have any insights into the problem.
4. Reach out to Atlassian support (if you're a paid customer) or submit a request for assistance through the Confluence Cloud help center.

I hope these suggestions help you identify and resolve the issue with your stuck export!
